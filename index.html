Abstract

Diffusion Policy has revolutionized robotic control with its ability to model complex multimodal distributions, yet its slow inference speed remains a critical bottleneck for real-time deployment.

We introduce Block-wise Adaptive Caching (BAC), a training-free acceleration framework designed specifically for Transformer-based Diffusion Policies. Unlike generic acceleration methods, BAC leverages the unique temporal redundancy of action features in robotic tasks. Experimental Setup

Real-world robot setup.

Task Description

We evaluate our method on the Pick-and-Release Task in a real-world setting. In this task, the robot should pick up a soft bag with a diameter of about 80% of the jaw width. This task greatly tests the real-time operating posture of the robotic arm and the timing of the gripper's opening and closing, as it is easy to knock over the bag.

Hardware Setup

  • GPU: NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090D (48 GB)
  • Robot Arm: Franka Research 3
  • Camera: UGREEN CM717
  • Data: 150 epsiodes collected by Gello
  • Baseline Model: Transformer-based Diffusion Policy (DP-T)
